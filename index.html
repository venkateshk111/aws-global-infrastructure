    Glossary
    Region
    A geographic cluster of AWS data centers made up of multiple isolated Availability Zones. You pick a region to control where your apps and data live.
    Availability Zone
    One or more data centers inside a region with independent power, cooling and networking. Spreading across AZs protects apps from single-facility failures.
    Local Zone
    A small extension of a region placed in a large metro area, bringing compute and storage within single-digit milliseconds of end users.
    Wavelength Zone
    AWS compute embedded inside a telecom carrier's 5G network, so mobile traffic reaches your app without leaving the carrier's network.
    Edge Location
    A CloudFront point of presence that caches content close to viewers to speed up delivery. AWS operates 750+ of them worldwide.
    Direct Connect
    A colocation facility where customers link their own network to AWS over dedicated private fiber instead of the public internet.
    Backbone Network
    AWS's private global fiber network — terrestrial and subsea — that carries traffic between regions and edge sites without touching the public internet.
